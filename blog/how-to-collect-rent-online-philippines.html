How to Collect Rent Online in the Philippines – 2026 Guide for Landlords

Published: June 2026 · By Yinzhen Technologies

Collecting rent in cash is a thing of the past. Between the traffic, the awkward follow‑ups, and the risk of lost bank deposit slips, it’s just not worth it. Fortunately, Filipino landlords now have several easy, secure ways to collect rent online — even from tenants who don’t have a credit card. This guide covers the best options and shows you how to automate the entire process.

1. GCash & Maya – The Most Accessible Options

GCash and Maya (formerly PayMaya) are the e‑wallets almost every Filipino already has. They are perfect for rent collection because:

  • Tenants can send money instantly using just your mobile number or QR code.
  • No bank account is needed on either side.
  • Both provide transaction receipts, so you have a digital paper trail.
  • They’re free for peer‑to‑peer transfers under certain limits.

The downside? You still need to manually check if a tenant has paid and match the payment to the correct unit. For a single condo, this is fine. For multiple units, it quickly becomes a spreadsheet nightmare.

2. Bank Transfers – Better Records, Slower Process

Online banking apps (BDO, BPI, UnionBank) also work. Tenants can transfer rent directly to your account. This provides a more official record, and you can easily track incoming payments via your bank statement. However, you’ll still need to manually link each deposit to a specific tenant and unit.

3. Xendit – The Professional Landlord’s Choice

Xendit is a payment gateway that acts as a middleman, but a trusted one. It’s the best option if you want to automate rent collection without holding tenant funds. Here’s how it works:

  • You create an invoice for each rent due.
  • The tenant gets a payment link. They can pay via GCash, Maya, card, or bank transfer — all in one checkout screen.
  • Xendit processes the payment and splits it: your chosen platform fee goes to the software provider, and the rest is instantly routed to your own Xendit sub‑account (meaning the software never touches your money).
  • Once paid, the system automatically marks the rent as paid in your dashboard.

That’s exactly how ManageYourCondo handles online rent collection. Each tenant sees a “Pay Now” button next to their rent balance, pays in seconds, and you wake up to a paid‑status update — no manual work required.

4. Which Method Should You Choose?

It depends on your portfolio size:

1–3 units: GCash or Maya alone is enough. Just keep a simple log.

4–10 units: Combine e‑wallets with a basic spreadsheet — or try a tool that slightly automates it.

10+ units: You need automation and a tenant portal. Otherwise, you’ll drown in manual tracking.
